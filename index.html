What You'll Learn Today

Today we're becoming emotional meteorologists - experts at reading our own inner weather and understanding the deeper climate needs beneath our feelings.

Primary Learning Target: "Emotional Needs Translation Skill" - The ability to identify underlying emotional needs beneath surface feelings and translate them into clear, connectable requests.

This skill builds authentic connection while reducing isolation and helping you communicate your needs directly rather than through behaviors.

Emotional Weather Station

Grounding & Present Moment Awareness

Step 1: Weather Check

Take three deep breaths with me... If your emotional state right now was weather, what would it be?

☀️ Sunny & Clear
☁️ Cloudy
⛈️ Stormy
🌫️ Foggy
🌧️ Light Rain
🌪️ Hurricane

Step 2: Climate vs. Weather

Weather = how you feel right now (anxious, excited, tired)

Climate = what you need consistently to thrive (safety, understanding, autonomy)

Weather is temporary, but climate tells us about deeper patterns.

Step 3: Body Compass Reading

Notice where you feel this weather in your body... And notice - underneath the weather, what does your system seem to be asking for?

The Translation Process

Key Teaching: Feelings are the messengers - they tell us about our needs. Needs are universal - we all share them.

Practice with a Real Example

Think of a recent moment when you felt frustrated with a friend...

Step 1: Notice the Weather (Feeling)

What am I experiencing emotionally right now?

Step 2: Find the Climate Need (Need)

What is this feeling telling me I need?

Step 3: Make a Clear Request

How can I ask for that need to be met?

"Real Talk" Moment

This is simple but not easy. Sometimes we've been trained to not even notice our needs, or to think they don't matter. Sometimes we might have gotten used to expressing needs through behaviors instead of words. That's normal and it makes sense - we're just learning a new language.
